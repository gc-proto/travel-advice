Before you go checklist

Check travel advice and advisories for detailed information on your destination. Check while you’re planning and again before you go in case there is new health or safety information that could affect your trip.

Register your travel so the Government of Canada can notify you in case of an emergency abroad or a personal emergency at home. Make sure you list all countries or ports of call (if you will be on a cruise).

Buy the best travel insurance you can. Your Canadian health insurance will not cover you when you are outside the country. Carry details of your travel health insurance with you on your trip. Make sure your policy includes accidental injury, hospitalization abroad and medical evacuation.

Make sure your vaccinations are up-to–date and ask for other advice on how to protect yourself from illness and injury. See a health care provider or visit a travel health clinic six weeks before you leave.

Carry a Canadian passport that is valid well past the date you expect to return to Canada. Keep a hidden copy of the identification page. Find out if you need a visa to enter your destination country. To enter the U.S. you must have a passport, a U. S. Passport Card, Enhanced Driver's Licence or a Trusted Traveler Program card (NEXUS, SENTRI and FAST).

If you are a dual citizen, check with your destination country’s embassy or consulate to avoid problems.

Leave copies of your passport identification page, itinerary and insurance policy with friends or family.

Check travel health tips, including how to eat and drink safely, and how to prevent insect bites.

Bring an extra supply of medication in case you are away longer than expected, and keep medication in original packaging.

  • Travelling with medication
  • Travelling with a medical device

If your child is travelling, make sure they carry documentation proving they have permission to travel abroad.

Plan your travel budget. Anticipate financial needs like local currency and departure tax. Keep extra funds in case of emergency.

If you are flying, find out about what you can bring on a plane. Check pre-boarding identification requirements and be familiar with the airport security screening process.

Check you mobile plan. When you use any mobile device in a foreign country, high roaming fees apply.

Get an international driving permit, if you need one.

Understand the risks of Alcohol, drugs and travel.

In case of emergency

Carry contact details for our Emergency Watch and Response Centre in Ottawa. It operates 24/7 and accepts collect calls (where available) from Canadians who need emergency help while abroad.
