Travel insurance


If you plan to travel outside Canada — even on a day trip to the United States — you should buy the best travel health insurance you can afford before you leave.

You may also wish to buy non-medical travel insurance. This can provide coverage for trip cancellation or interruption, flight delays, and lost or stolen baggage.

Why you should buy travel health insurance 

  • Your Canadian health insurance is generally not valid outside Canada
  • Your provincial or territorial health plan may cover nothing or only a small part of the costs of medical care abroad, and will never pay bills up front
  • Foreign hospitals can be very expensive, and may require immediate cash payment
  • In some countries hospitals and clinics refuse to treat patients who do not have enough insurance or money to pay their bills
  • The Government of Canada will not pay your medical bills

What travel health insurance should cover 

Medical evacuation

Make sure the policy covers medical evacuation to Canada or to the nearest location with appropriate medical care. The policy should also cover the costs of a medical escort to accompany you to your final destination.

Pre-existing medical conditions

Ask the company to explain the definition, limitations and restrictions of any pre-existing conditions and tests and treatments you may have undergone.

  • Make sure you get a written agreement that your pre-existing medical condition is covered, or you could find your claim “null and void” under a pre-existing condition clause.
  • Be aware that the agreement must also include a stability clause stating that for you to be covered for any pre-existing medical conditions you must have no changes to or new medical conditions, symptoms or medications during the stability period prior to your trip.
  • The agreement should include a compassion clause stating that an inaccurate statement may not invalidate the entire policy, and a change of health clause.

Repatriation in case of death

Verify that the plan covers the preparation and return of your remains to Canada.

Know what you're buying

Carefully research your needs. Verify the terms, conditions, limitations, exclusions and requirements of your insurance policy before you leave Canada.

When assessing a travel health insurance plan, you should ask a lot of questions.

  • Is there a deductible, and how much is it?
  • Does the plan provide continuous coverage for the duration of your stay abroad and after you return?
  • Does it offer coverage that is renewable from abroad and for the maximum period of stay?
  • Does the company have an in-house, worldwide, 24-hour/7-day emergency contact number in English and/or translation services for health care providers in your destination country?
  • Does it pay for foreign hospitalization for illness or injury and related medical costs?
  • Does it provide up-front payment of bills or cash advances, so you don’t have to pay out of your own pocket?

Meet the terms of your policy

It is your responsibility to know and understand the terms of your insurance policy. Read the fine print carefully and ask for help, if necessary.

The information you provide must be accurate and complete. If you have any questions, contact the insurance company. Ask them to clarify the issue in writing.

Get approval from your insurer before undergoing medical treatment. Travel health insurance rarely covers routine health checkups, non-emergency care and cosmetic surgery. Insurance companies may also exclude coverage for mental health disorders, drug- or alcohol-related incidents, or extreme sporting activities such as bungee jumping and rock climbing.

Get a detailed report and invoice from the doctor or hospital before leaving the country where you have received medical treatment. There is nothing more frustrating than trying to get the proper paperwork from thousands of kilometres away. Always submit the original receipts for medical services or prescriptions you received abroad. Keep a copy of the documents for your files.

Carry details of your insurance policy and tell your travel agent, a travel companion, and a friend or relative at home how to contact your insurer.

Check for travel advisories

Your insurance company may not honour medical claims if the Government of Canada has advised that you should avoid travel to that destination.

Check the advice about your destination in advance, and check again just before you go.

Destination advice and advisories

Where you can get travel insurance

You can buytravel insurance through your travel agent, insurance broker or your employer’s insurance provider. Your credit card company may also offer travel and health insurance.

Travel safe

  • Carry details of your insurance policy and tell your travel agent, a travel companion, and a friend or relative at home how to contact your insurer
  • Register with us, and include your insurance policy details, such as the name of the insurance provider and policy number when signing up
  • Check the destination advice and advisories twice for each destination as it could affect your insurance coverage
  • Call the nearest Canadian embassy or consulate for emergency assistance or reach the Emergency Watch and Response Centre 24/7 by telephone at +1 613 996-8885 (call collect where available) or email at sos@international.gc.ca
